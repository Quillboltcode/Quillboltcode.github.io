> [!NOTE]
> This is a curated reading list for my research on thinking mechanisms in LLMs, particularly focusing on Continuous Thought Machines (CTM).

---

## Must Read

### Darlow et al. - Continuous Thought Machines (2025)
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
CTM's architecture, dynamic reasoning, and adaptive computation  
[Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.05522)

### Sun et al. - Learning to (Learn at Test Time) (2024/2025)
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Test-time training and dynamic hidden states  
[Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.04620)

### Behrouz et al. - Test-Time Memorization (TTM) / Titans (2025/2026)
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Test-time adaptation, memory caching, and associative memory  
[Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.00663)

### Zhao and Jones - Fast-weight Product Key Memory (FwPKM) (2026)
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Sparse fast-weight memory for test-time learning  
[Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.00671)

### Geiping et al. - Scaling up Test-Time Compute (2025)
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Recurrent depth and latent reasoning for test-time adaptation  
[Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.05171)

### Test-Time Training for Abstract Reasoning (2025)
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Test-time adaptation for ARC-AGI  
[Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.07279)

### Houlsby et al. - Adapter Modules (2019)
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Plug-and-play adapters for efficient transfer learning  
[Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.00751)

### Pfeiffer et al. - AdapterFusion (2020)
⭐⭐⭐
Dynamic composition of adapters  
[Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.00247)

### Hu et al. - LoRA (2021)
⭐⭐⭐
Low-rank adaptation for efficient fine-tuning  
[Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.09685)

---

## Should Read

### Banino et al. - PonderNet: Learning to Ponder (2021)
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Introduces adaptive computation in neural networks, similar to CTM's internal ticks. Useful for understanding how to design dynamic reasoning modules.  
[Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.05407)

### Graves - Adaptive Computation Time (ACT) for RNNs (2016)
⭐⭐⭐⭐
A seminal paper on adaptive computation for RNNs. Core feature of CTM - helps contextualize CTM's design within the broader literature.  
[Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/1603.08983)

### Zhu et al. - Scaling Latent Reasoning via Looped Language Models (2025)
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Pre-trained Loop Language Models (LoopLM) with iterative computation in latent space. Builds reasoning into pre-training phase.  
[Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25741)
